A* Search

Imagine you are standing at the yellow square and want to walk the easiest path possible to the orange square. However, between you and the destination are grassy patches (green squares) which are tiring to walk through, and deep puddles (blue squares) which you absolutely do not want to go through.


How will you ever find the path? Well, let the A* Search algorithm calculate it for you. The algorithm utilizes the "cost" of the path to determine the best path efficiently.


In one sentence: The computer remembers the cost of each square (grass vs. normal and how far it is from the destination) and visits neighboring squares with the lowest costs until the destination is reached

Step by step:
1. Initialize a list called frontier that contains pairs of (square, total_cost). Let frontier be ordered from lowest total_cost to highest total_cost.
-Let the cost of black_square = 1, green_square = 5, blue_square = infinity, and path_cost be the sum of the costs of all squares on the path.
-Let manhattan_dist = vertical_distance + horizontal_distance between the current square and destination
-Let total_cost = path_cost + manhattan_dist

2. Start at the yellow square.

3.For every neighboring square:
-If the square is the destination, congratulations! You have found the shortest path.
-If the square has not been explored, add the (square, total_cost) pair to frontier.
-If the square has been explored, if the total_cost is less than the current total_cost in frontier, add the (square, total_cost) pair to frontier.

4. Take the first pair from frontier (the one with the lowest cost), and repeat step 3 until the destination is found.
